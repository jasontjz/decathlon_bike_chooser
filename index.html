Hybrid Cycling

If you can't decide whether you will spend more time riding on the road and PCNs or if you might want to go somewhere a little more rugged, instead of choosing between a road bike or a mountain bike, why not choose a Hybrid bike?

A hybrid bike is a mix between a road bike, mountain bike and even a city bike. The frame is designed for the rider to sit upright like on a city bike and not as aggressive as a road or even mountain bike. The tyres are wider and knobbier than a road bike for more ruggedness, yet skinnier and smoother than a mountain bike for better rolling speed on the roads. On some models, the bike is equipped with a suspension fork to smooth out bumps, yet does not bounce as much as on a mountain bike in order to achieve the perfect balance between comfort and efficiency. And while it is not equipped with a rack by default like on a city bike, the frame has the correct mounting points and Decathlon Singapore offers a wide choice of racks to choose from (available to purchase separately). It is truly a hybrid in all senses of the word, offering you the best of all the cycling worlds!

Road Endurance Cycling

Road Endurance Cycling prioritize long distances, and the bikes feature comfortable geometry, sturdiness and practical features like rack mounts.
Road endurance riders are typically riding not only to clock miles, but to enjoy the sights and sounds along the way.
The average pace over a 2 hour ride is usually around 20-30km/h and may include a stop or two.
Road endurance riders will also enjoy participating in Gran Fondos or Century rides in a large group, with an emphasis on finishing the ride as opposed to coming in first.
